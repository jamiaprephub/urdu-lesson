Lesson 1 · Q1 · 10 Marks
دو چشمی ہ
Do Chashmii He — The Aspirated "H" (ہ)
What is دو چشمی ہ?
This special form of ہ is called do chashmii he ("two-eyed he") because of its two loop-like openings when written in the middle of a word (ﻬ). It is NOT pronounced as a standalone "h." Instead, it attaches to the consonant before it to create an aspirated sound — a consonant with a puff of breath.

In Roman transliteration, aspirated consonants are written as two-letter combinations: bh, ph, th, Th, jh, chh, dh, Dh, Rh, kh, gh. In Urdu script, all of these are written as: consonant + ہ (do chashmii he).

Formula: ب + ہ = بھ (bh) · پ + ہ = پھ (ph) · گ + ہ = گھ (gh) · ک + ہ = کھ (kh) etc.
💡
Key distinction (from textbook): Capital Roman letters like GH = غ and KH = خ refer to the Arabic letters غین and خے (throat sounds). Lowercase with h like gh = گھ and kh = کھ are do chashmii he aspirated consonants.

Lesson 1 · Q3 · 10 Marks
اضافت
Izafat — The Possessive Construction
What is اضافت?
Izafat (اضافت) is a grammatical construction borrowed from Persian. It connects two nouns to show a possessive or descriptive relationship — like "of" in English. It is written as a small "ے" (or "-e-") sound between two words.

Pattern: [Noun 1] + ے + [Noun 2] = "[Noun 2] of [Noun 1]"
Example: درد دل = dard-e-dil = "pain of the heart" (heartache)

In written Urdu, izafat is often shown with a small zaer (zer) under the first noun's last letter, or by a hyphen in Roman script. In speech it is the "-e-" linking sound.

Writing rule: When the first word ends in ہ or ة, the izafat is written as ۂ (he with hamza). E.g. شعبہ + اردو = شعبۂ اردو (shuba-e-urdu). When it ends in ا or و, izafat sounds like "-e-" or "-ye-": دریا + گنگا = دریائے گنگا.
Lesson 2 · Q4 · 10 Marks
واوِ عطف
Vaao Atf — The Connective "And" (و)
What is واو عطف?
In Persian and classical Urdu, the letter و is used as a short, elegant connector meaning "and" between two nouns. This is called vaao-e-atf (عطف = connection/conjunction). It creates compound nouns or paired phrases commonly used in poetry and literary prose.

Everyday Urdu uses اور for "and." Classical/poetic Urdu uses واو عطف. The و is pronounced as a light "-o-" or "-u-" sound linking the two words.

Pattern: خاص و عام = khaas-o-aam (special and common)

Lesson 2 · Q5 · 10 Marks
واوِ معدُولہ
Vaao Madula — The Silent Wao
What is واو معدولہ?
"Madula" (معدولہ) means deviated or suppressed. The واو معدولہ is a و written in a word but not fully pronounced — its vowel sound is swallowed or greatly reduced.

It occurs mainly in Arabic-Persian borrowed words where خ is followed by و. The و is present in writing but the full "aw/o" sound is not given. These are exception words you must memorise.

Examples from textbook (page 83): خواب، خواجہ، خود، خوش، خورشید، دسترخوان
All start with خو — the و after خ in this group behaves as vaao madula.

الف مقصورہ
Alif Maqsura — The Shortened Alif
What is الف مقصورہ?
"Maqsura" (مقصورہ) means shortened. It is a form of Alif that is written as ی (without dots) at the end of a word, but pronounced as a long "ā" sound — exactly like the regular Alif.

It always carries the small superscript mark ٰ (madd/khara zabar) above it to signal that this ی is actually an "ā" sound, not an "ee/ii" sound.

Rule: The mark ٰ above ی at the end of a word = alif maqsura = pronounce "ā"
These words are all borrowed from Arabic.

Khra Zer (کھڑا زیر) is a special diacritical mark used in some Arabic words. It looks like a small vertical line below a letter (resembling a standing zaer). It marks an ʿain-like sound or long vowel in certain religious/classical words.

Lesson 2 · Q7 · 10 Marks
تنوین (دو زَبر)
Tanwin — The Double Zabar (-an ending)
What is تنوین?
"Tanwin" (تنوین) comes from Arabic grammar. It is the double zabar mark ( ً ) placed above the last letter of a word, making it end with an "-an" sound. In Urdu, tanwin words are always Arabic adverbs that are used as intensifiers or qualifiers in sentences.

Symbol: Two zabar marks ( ً ) placed above the final letter
Pronunciation: The word gains an "-an" ending sound
Function: Often used as adverbs meaning "immediately," "certainly," "approximately," etc.

Lesson 1 · Q8 · 10 Marks
الف لام کا استعمال
Alif-Lam — The Definite Article in Compound Words
What is الف لام?
In Arabic, ال (alif + lam) is the definite article meaning "the." When Arabic compound words are used in Urdu, this ال becomes part of the word. There are two types:

1. لام قمری (Lam Qamari): The "l" of ال is pronounced. This happens when ال comes before certain letters (called "moon letters"). Example: بالکل (bilkul = entirely), القصہ (al-qissa = the story).

2. لام شمسی (Lam Shamsi): The "l" of ال is not pronounced; instead the following letter doubles. This happens with "sun letters" (ت، ث، د، ذ، ر، ز، س، ش، ص، ض، ط، ظ، ل، ن). Example: نظام الدین → pronounced nizaamud-diin (the ل is replaced by doubling the د).

Lesson 2 · Q9 · 10 Marks
رومن / دیوناگری
Transliteration — Roman & Devnagri
What is transliteration?
Transliteration means writing a word from one script into another script, keeping the sound the same. You are NOT translating the meaning — only the script changes. The textbook uses Roman transliteration throughout to help learners.

Roman Transliteration: Writing Urdu sounds using English alphabet letters.
Devnagri Transliteration: Writing Urdu sounds using Hindi script.

Challenge: Urdu has sounds that do not exist in Hindi/English (ق، خ، ع، غ، ح، ط، ظ، ص، ض) so we use capital letters or dots to indicate them.

Ikhtisaraat — Abbreviations in Urdu
What are اختصارات?
"Ikhtisaraat" means abbreviations — short symbols placed after names or dates to convey information concisely. The textbook (pages 88–89) lists abbreviations used in religious, literary, and date contexts. You must know the symbol and what it stands for.
ﷺ
Symbol

For Prophet Muhammad ﷺ

Written after the name of the Prophet. Stands for Sallallahu Alayhi Wa Sallam (Peace and blessings be upon him).

صلی اللہ علیہ وسلم
ؓ
Symbol

For Companions of the Prophet (Sahaaba)

Written after names of the Prophet's Companions. Stands for Radi Allahu Anhu/Anha (May Allah be pleased with him/her).

رضی اللہ عنہ / عنہا
ؒ
Symbol

For Muslim Deceased Saints

Written after names of deceased Muslim scholars/saints. Stands for Rahmatullah Alayhi (May Allah have mercy on him).

رحمۃ اللہ علیہ
تخلص
Symbol

Takhallus — Poet's Pen Name

In Urdu poetry, a poet's takhallus (pen name) is indicated before writing the name. Example: Mirza Asadullah Beg Khan, تخلص: غالب

تخلص کا نشان
؎
Symbol

Before Writing a Verse (Sher)

The symbol ؎ (alamat-e-sher) is placed before a quoted poetic verse in prose to signal that a couplet follows.

علامتِ شعر
ھ
Symbol

Hijri Year (ہجری)

Written after a year number to indicate the Islamic/Hijri calendar year. Example: ۱۴۴۵ ھ = Year 1445 Hijri. Stands for ہجری.

ہجری کیلنڈر کے وَرش کا سوچک — ۱۵۰۰ ھ = ؁
سن (یعنی)
Christian Year

Christian / Common Era Year

Written for the Christian calendar (Isvi year). The abbreviation ء or سن after a year number indicates Common Era. Example: ۲۰۲۴ء = year 2024 CE.

اِسوی کیلنڈر کے وَرش کا سوچک

Unit I

Do Chashmi He (ہ): Makes previous consonant aspirated — بھ، پھ، تھ، جھ، چھ، دھ، کھ، گھ، ڑھ، ٹھ
Letter Groups: fe/qaaf (ف ق) · ain/ghain (ع غ) · suaad/zuaad (ص ض) · toe/zoe (ط ظ)
Capital Roman: GH=غ · KH=خ (Arabic throat letters)

Unit II

Izafat (اضافت): "-e-" connecting two nouns showing possession — دردِ دل، خوفِ خدا
Vaao Atf (واو عطف): و as "and" in poetry — صبح و شام، گل و بلبل
Vaao Madula (واو معدولہ): Silent و in خو- words — خواب، خواجہ، خود

Unit III

Alif Maqsura: ٰی at end of word sounds like "ā" — اَعلیٰ، اَدنیٰ، دَعویٰ، فَتویٰ، موسیٰ، عیسیٰ
Tanwin (دو زبر): ( ً ) adds "-an" sound — فوراً، تقریباً، مثلاً، غالباً

Unit IV
